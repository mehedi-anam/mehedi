01About

Field surveys, molecular tools, and the policy that follows.

Based at
Communicable Disease Control
DGHS, Dhaka

Collaborating with
WHO · APMEN · GFATM
IEDCR · IUCN

For six years I have led Bangladesh's national vector-surveillance response to dengue, chikungunya, malaria and kala-azar — coordinating 100+ entomological surveys a year across high-risk districts and training hundreds of health workers in the process.

My hands-on work runs from field (larval and adult mosquito sampling, ovitrap deployment, GIS mapping of transmission hotspots) to bench (qRT-PCR, Vgsc / kdr genotyping, WHO-standard susceptibility bioassays). I co-authored the 4th and 5th editions of the National Dengue Prevention Guidelines and contributed to the National Strategic Plan for Malaria Elimination — translating field data into the policies that shape how 170 million people are protected.

I'm now seeking a PhD position in South Korea where I can build deeper molecular and computational expertise — integrating insecticide-resistance genomics, spatial epidemiology, and One Health frameworks to address emerging vector-borne threats in South and East Asia.
